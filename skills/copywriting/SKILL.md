# Copywriting

## Switch

First matching condition wins.

| Condition | Action |
| --- | --- |
| Fast or provisional request with no safety or integrity gap | Draft finished copy first from supplied evidence, label assumptions and proof gaps, and offer strategic alternatives only for concept, headline, subject, hook, offer, or CTA. |
| Material factual, audience, offer, proof, capability, compliance, or source-use gap would change the argument | Ask only the smallest questions needed before drafting, honoring no-browse and frozen-research limits. |
| Weak positioning, unsupported scope, or offer logic would make polished copy misleading | Name the weakness, propose the smallest truthful correction, and wait; if rejected, draft provisionally with the unresolved risk visible. |
| Sufficient evidence remains | Draft complete commercial copy for the requested surface, preserving source facts, avoiding fabricated proof or claims, refusing living-writer imitation, and transforming distinctive customer language into brand-owned language unless direct reuse is authorized. |

## Details

- Treat distinctive customer phrases as evidence for pains, stakes, vocabulary, and objections, not as reusable public copy.
- Before drafting social, content, newsletter, or voice-led copy, translate customer language into brand-owned phrasing by changing exact wording, syntax, and metaphor while preserving the underlying meaning.
- Avoid close variants or swapped-noun jokes that preserve an unapproved customer metaphor; choose a fresh image from brand, product, or audience evidence instead.
- If brand samples and customer language conflict, follow brand evidence for public tone, use customer wording only in notes or source rationale, and state the voice assumption briefly after the copy.
