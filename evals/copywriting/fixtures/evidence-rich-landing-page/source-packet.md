# Fixture: evidence-rich landing page

## User request

Rewrite our landing page. We have enough source material and want a polished
first draft, not a strategy memo.

## Company

Gridline Flow is a fictional operations-planning product for regional
fulfillment teams. It connects warehouse schedules, carrier pickups, and
customer promise dates in one planning view.

## Objective

Primary action: book a 30-minute operations review.
Business metric: qualified demo requests from VP Operations and Directors of
Fulfillment at companies with 3-20 distribution sites.

## Traffic context

Paid search and retargeting traffic from queries such as "reduce missed
carrier pickups" and "warehouse schedule planning software." Visitors usually
know the problem but do not know Gridline Flow.

## Audience and situation

- Owns fulfillment performance across multiple sites.
- Uses spreadsheets, carrier portals, WMS exports, and daily standups.
- Feels the pain when late pickup decisions are discovered after cut-off.
- Alternatives: spreadsheets, WMS reports, a BI dashboard, hiring more planners,
  or asking carriers for static pickup windows.

## Current hero copy

Title: "The intelligent platform for modern logistics teams"
Subtitle: "Optimize your operations with AI-powered workflows."
CTA: "Get started"

## Differentiated facts

- Shows warehouse labor plans, carrier pickup windows, and order promise dates
  on one timeline.
- Imports CSV exports from fictional WMS tools BoxDock, PalletTrail, and North
  Yard.
- Flags orders at risk of missing pickup before the carrier cut-off.
- Lets managers simulate moving a labor block or splitting a carrier load.
- Implementation is a two-week assisted setup for teams that already export
  daily order and labor files.

## Proof inventory

- Pilot with fictional customer Cedar & Co. reduced same-day expedite requests
  from 42 to 29 in four weeks after a two-site rollout.
- Internal product benchmark: planners can load a 2,000-order CSV and produce a
  risk report in under 90 seconds on the sample dataset.
- Cedar & Co. quote, approved for use: "The timeline made the pickup risk
  visible before the floor felt it."
- Screenshot available: multi-site timeline with red pickup-risk markers.

## Objections

- "Will this replace our WMS?" No. It sits above WMS exports for planning.
- "Will carriers need to use it?" No. Carrier windows are imported or entered by
  the team.
- "Is this a six-month implementation?" No. Assisted setup is two weeks if the
  required exports exist.
- "Does it automate labor decisions?" No. It surfaces risk and simulations;
  managers decide.

## Voice constraints

Plain, senior-operator language. Avoid startup hype, "AI magic," "autonomous,"
"revolutionary," and unsupported category leadership claims.

## Required output

Finished copy first:
- Hero headline, subhead, CTA, and above-fold proof line.
- Five body sections with headings and copy.
- FAQ with three questions.
- One repeated CTA block.
- Three strategic headline alternatives.
- Two CTA alternatives.

After copy, add concise notes for assumptions, missing evidence, risky claims,
and test hypotheses.
