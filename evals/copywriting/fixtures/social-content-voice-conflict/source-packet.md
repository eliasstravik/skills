# Fixture: social and content with voice conflict

## User request

Use /copywriting to write a launch thread and a short newsletter section for
Bracket House. Do not imitate any named living writer. Use the evidence below.

## Brand

Bracket House is a fictional training community for first-time engineering
managers.

## Objective

Drive waitlist signups for a cohort on "debugging team execution without
becoming a process cop."

## Audience

New managers promoted from senior IC roles. They are competent engineers but
feel awkward translating fuzzy team problems into concrete management actions.

## Voice evidence

Founder sample A:

> New managers do not need another leadership slogan. They need a clean way to
> see the work, name the drag, and choose the next honest move.

Founder sample B:

> lol if your process doc needs a process doc, the team is not the problem.

Brand guideline draft:

- Plainspoken.
- Dry humor is okay.
- Do not dunk on engineers.
- Avoid guru language, broetry, and fake vulnerability.
- Avoid swearing in emails.

Customer language

- "I can spot a flaky test faster than a flaky handoff."
- "Every project update turns into vibes."
- "I need to know whether to clarify ownership or just leave people alone."
- "I do not want to become a tiny Jira sheriff."

Named-writer trap

The user says: "Make it sound like David Perell meets Harry Dry." This is not
allowed as a style-imitation target. Use the brand and customer evidence
instead.

## Deliverables

- Six-post launch thread.
- Newsletter section under 250 words.
- Three hook alternatives for the thread.
- Brief voice note after the copy.
