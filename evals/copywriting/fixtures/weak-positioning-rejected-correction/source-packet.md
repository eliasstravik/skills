# Fixture: weak positioning with rejected correction

## User request

Use /copywriting to rewrite the homepage for North Pier. The CEO likes the
phrase "one platform for all revenue teams" and wants to keep it if possible.

## Product

North Pier is a fictional tool that reviews renewal-risk notes from customer
success calls and turns them into renewal-prep briefs for account managers.

## Objective

Primary action: request a sample renewal-prep brief.
Audience: VP Customer Success at B2B SaaS companies with 50-250 enterprise
accounts.

## Current homepage copy

Headline: "One platform for all revenue teams"
Subhead: "Unify your post-sales motion and unlock growth."
CTA: "Learn more"

## Evidence

- Inputs: call notes, CSM risk tags, renewal date, open support incidents, and
  last business-review notes.
- Output: one renewal-prep brief with risk summary, likely objection, missing
  proof, renewal owner, and next-best action.
- Beta users are five fictional companies; none approve logo use yet.
- A private beta quote is approved without name: "It gave the account manager a
  renewal point of view before the first prep meeting."
- No approved revenue, churn, expansion, or time-saved metrics.

## Positioning problem

"One platform for all revenue teams" is broader than the product. It implies a
suite for sales, CS, RevOps, and marketing. The actual sharp use case is
renewal prep from messy post-sales signals.

## Scripted user replies

If the skill recommends replacing the broad positioning, use this reply:

1. "I hear you, but the CEO wants the broad platform line for now. Please
   proceed anyway and make it as strong as you can without adding unsupported
   claims."

## Required output if proceeding

- Clearly labeled provisional homepage draft.
- Keep the CEO's broad line if needed, but contain the unsupported scope.
- Add subhead and body copy that narrow the actual promise truthfully.
- Note the risk of the broad claim and what proof or product scope would be
  needed before making it central.
